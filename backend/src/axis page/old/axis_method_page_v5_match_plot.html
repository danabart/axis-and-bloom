The Axis

Every taste has
a location.

Flavor isn't subjective noise — it's signal. The Axis maps every coffee and every palate to the same seven-dimensional sensory space. Matching means finding how close those two points are.

Step 1 — the flavor space

Five regions. One continuous map.

A 2D chart hides the differences that matter most. Parallel coordinates show how all seven dimensions separate the archetypes at once.

What about decaf?

Decaf is a preference layer, not an archetype. A Chocolate & Nutty drinker who prefers decaf gets the same vector — the decaf flag is passed separately to the AI sommelier.

What about experimental coffees?

Experimental processing amplifies flavor but doesn't create a new archetype. Dimension scores still place the coffee within one of the five regions — tagged experimental, matched as usual.

Step 2 — the seven dimensions

A coffee is a vector, not a word.

Each coffee's position is built from three overlapping sources, aggregated via the Collaborative Flavor Wheel:

Trained cuppers — SCA-scored sessions, 0–15 per dimension.
Roastery notes — bag descriptors mapped to the SCA wheel.
Customer feedback — post-delivery tasting notes, weighted by volume and recency.

Vectors evolve continuously

As community feedback accumulates, every coffee's position in flavor space is recalibrated. A vector from six months ago reflects what a hundred drinkers have said since.
